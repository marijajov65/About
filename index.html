My Story

My path from college athlete to software engineer

01

The Foundation

2017 - 2022
At DePaul, I learned that the same focus I used on the tennis court worked in computer science classes too. Managing a full course load while training and competing taught me how to prioritize and work efficiently.

Ended up with a 4.0 GPA and some nice recognition, but more importantly, I figured out that I genuinely enjoyed solving technical problems and building things.
02

The Exploration

2019 - 2020
Two internships taught me different things. At Wintrust, I saw how software affects real people's finances, which made me care more about getting things right. The IAESTE exchange to Serbia was unexpected - working on medical applications in a different culture showed me how technology problems are universal.

Both experiences pushed me out of my comfort zone and helped me understand that good software isn't just about the code - it's about the people using it.
03

The Evolution

2022 - Present
At Gelber Group, I work on systems where performance actually matters. Trading happens fast, data streams are constant, and if something breaks, people notice immediately.

I've built desktop trading interfaces, web dashboards for risk management, and algorithms that distribute computing resources fairly. It's technical work that has real impact, and I like that combination of challenge and responsibility.

Financial Technology
